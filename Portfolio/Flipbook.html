Plant Journal

Venus Flytrap

Native to the Carolinas (USA), this plant uses jaw-like leaves to trap unsuspecting bugs. The "jaws" close shut when tiny hairs are triggered.

Sarracenia

These North American pitcher plants lure insects with nectar, guiding them into a slippery tube where they fall in and can't escape.

Nepenthes

Known as tropical pitcher plants or "monkey cups," these species grow in Southeast Asia and have fluid-filled traps to digest prey.

Drosera

Also called sundews, these sticky plants trap insects with glistening glandular hairs that curl around their prey.

Pinguicula

Also known as butterworts, these carnivorous cuties use sticky, greasy leaves to trap tiny insects. Once caught, the leaf rolls slightly to digest the prey.
